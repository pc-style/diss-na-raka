# Diss na raka hits 122M PLN peak — what’s verified, what’s next

## Executive Summary
The "Diss na raka" charity livestream organized by Łatwogang for Fundacja Cancer Fighters concluded its nine-day run with historic success, though the financial reporting requires careful reconciliation. While social media claims suggest a total of 124 million PLN, this figure remains unverified [verification_status_of_124m_claim[0]][1]. The highest credible media-reported peak from the live on-stream counter is approximately 122 million PLN [executive_summary[0]][1]. This live counter aggregated gross donations across multiple channels (Siepomaga, Tipply, YouTube), whereas the official Siepomaga platform snapshot showed 24,052,004 PLN at a specific timepoint [executive_summary[4]][2]. The campaign's massive momentum was driven by a strategic ladder of milestones, high-profile celebrity reconciliations, symbolic head-shaving stunts, and significant corporate donations. Moving forward, the immediate priority is to publish a reconciliation plan detailing the net-range estimate after platform fees and holds, shifting the narrative from unverified gross peaks to audited net impact.

## Controversies and Brand Safety

### Learning from the Pasibus incident
The campaign faced a brand safety issue when fast-food chain Pasibus pledged 60,000 PLN on the condition that Doda perform at their festival [controversies_and_criticisms.controversy_title[0]][12]. This conditional offer sparked public backlash for being "marketing-driven charity." Pasibus subsequently apologized and made an unconditional donation of 100,000 PLN [controversies_and_criticisms.resolution_or_outcome[0]][13].

*Takeaway: Enforce a strict "no-strings" donor policy and pre-clear activation concepts to prevent live-time missteps.*
